What's the problem?

Distracted driving is a serious and rapidly growing threat to road safety, and with more and more people owning phones, this problem is likely to escalate significantly in coming years.

Studies from a number of countries have suggested that the proportion of drivers using mobile phones while driving has increased significantly compared to 5-10 years ago, ranging from 1% top up to 11% at any one point in time.

Using mobile phones causes drivers to take their eyes off the road, their hands off the steering wheel, and distracts their mind from the road and whatâ€™s around them. Studies suggest that using a mobile phone while driving makes teenagers four times more at risk of a crash than someone not using a phone, which is similar to, if not higher than the risks of driving while intoxicated.

We have become far too reliant upon our mobile devices, which makes it hard to avoid using them while driving. However, there are some ways that you can minimize the risk of this occuring, as outlined below.

What can I do?

According to an AAA (Australian Automobile Association) poll, 94% of teen drivers acknowledge the dangers of texting and driving, but 35% admitted to doing it anyway. You can prevent this by putting your phone on silent before you start driving, which will minimize the chance of any distractions occurring while you are driving.

You can also purposefully place your phone out of reach of yourself while driving, for example in the backseat, to avoid the tempation of having it close to you. If you do need to use the phone, make sure that you pull over in a safe area to use it.

Another way to stop those around you from using their phone while driving, is to make them aware of the punishments associated with using a phone while driving. In Canberra, if you are found to be using your mobile phone to text or use social media, you will be liable to pay up to a $480 fine.
